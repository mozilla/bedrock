Thank you for your interest in Mozilla QA. Please respond to this email indicating what areas of QA you would like to help out with. A QA team member will followup with you personally to help get you started with Mozilla QA. Below is some information to get you started with Mozilla QA.

Are you interested in testing desktop firefox? Then, take a look at our team wiki here - https://wiki.mozilla.org/QA/Desktop_Firefox to find out what we do. Looking to help us out right now? Then, take a look at our getting involved page here - https://wiki.mozilla.org/QA/Desktop_Firefox/GetInvolved to find something you would like to do and reply to this email telling us what you are interested in.

Are you interested in testing mobile firefox?  Then, take a look at our team wiki here - https://wiki.mozilla.org/QA/Fennec to find out about all the things the mobile QA team does when testing Firefox on various mobile devices. Looking to help us out right now? Then, take a look at our getting inolved page here - https://wiki.mozilla.org/QA/Fennec/NativeUI#Get_involved.21 to find out an activitiy that you wish to check out. Are you more Web-centric?  Want to learn, or already know Selenium, and other Web-testing frameworks and automation?  Then take a look at our team wiki here - https://wiki.mozilla.org/QA/Execution/Web_Testing to find out more about what we do, and please reach out to us via your preferred method from https://quality.mozilla.org/teams/web-qa/, and say Hi!

Are you interested in writing test automation? Want to learn about how we test desktop firefox in an automated fashion? Then check out our automation development wiki here - https://wiki.mozilla.org/QA/Automation_Services for more information about what our automation development team does and the projects we are involved in.

Looking to join some of our community testing events? Join us every Friday for Test Days which run from 1am to 5pm PDT. Please reference https://quality.mozilla.org/category/events/ which lists all of the upcoming events. If you check the test etherpad before the event, you can familiarize yourself with the test topic and do any necessary setup so you are ready to test when the event occurs.

To get started, join the #testday IRC channel here - http://chat.mibbit.com/?server=irc.mozilla.org&channel=#testday and let us know that you would like to help. We have moderators in the IRC channel who are more than happy to help and answer any questions you may have. We look forward to your participation and contributions!
