Diversity & Inclusion at Mozilla: 2022 Results

Introduction

As an ever-expanding organization with employees, volunteers and community members spanning the globe, we strive to continue the journey in our commitment to fostering an equitable and inclusive culture — while also maintaining alignment to our core organizational values of transparency and accountability. We encourage you all to join our movement.

At Mozilla we operate cohesively under five separate but unified entities: the Foundation (MoFo), Corporation (MoCo), MZLA, Mozilla Ventures, and Mozilla.ai. Our annual disclosure gives us a unified opportunity as ‘One Mozilla’ to both reflect on our ambitious vision of fostering an equitable culture that enables inclusion, and to remain activated in our core organizational values of transparency and accountability around the commitments we’ve made. Historically, we have reported information on the Foundation and Corporation and are aiming to begin including information on our newest entities in future reports. This year's report will continue to focus on the Foundation and Corporation.

In 2022, on the Corporation side, we re-oriented ourselves foundationally and strategically formalized our new Inclusion, Diversity, Equity and Accountability (IDEA) team which sits within our broader Social and Environmental Impact (SEI) team. This created a strategic runway for us to continue building on the momentum from 2021.

Each year we aim to account for our progress and challenges in advancing our commitment to being a diverse, equitable and inclusive organization driven by accountability. This 2023 disclosure captures our progress through 2022. This year, we are pleased to continue providing data that captures the intersectional experiences of our employees in recognition of the complexity of our identities.

Lastly, as a growing global organization, we acknowledge that the racial and ethnic representation data we’re disclosing is specific to the United States. We are looking to develop better global reporting that is reflective of our Mozillians (for contextual reference, at MoCo 64% of our employee population is based in the U.S., while only 40% of our MoFo employees are based in the U.S.), and also complies with global privacy and employment laws while providing more representative data and insights. We’ve made strides in some areas of representation and remained stagnant in other areas. There are useful lessons to be learned from the goals we met and missed, and we are eager to continue our journey.

Looking back: progress across Mozilla

Since reporting our representation metrics in 2018, we continue to monitor positive progress in advancing gender and racial/ethnic diversity. In 2022, women represented 38% of Mozilla Corporation’s employee population — an almost 9-point increase from 2018. At the Mozilla Foundation, the progress continues with women representing more than 70% of the total employee population amounting to an almost 6-point increase in women from 2018.

When we look at the racial and ethnic representation over the last five years, we have made progress and also recognize opportunities for hiring and retention. Mozilla Corporation saw a 5.2 percentage point increase in Black/African American representation from 2018, and the Mozilla Foundation saw a 12.1 percentage point increase in Black/African American representation from 2018.
