{{ _('How ad blockers work') }}

{{ _('A typical ad blocker') }}

{{ _('Most ad blockers work in two ways.') }}

{{ _('First, they block communication from ad servers or others. Ads simply can’t get through.') }}

{{ _('Second, they hide parts of a web page that could be ads-- any section of a site that’s labeled "ad," for example. Most ad blockers use public and up-to-date lists of known ad servers.') }}

{{ _('The Firefox ad blocker') }}

{% trans url='https://blog.mozilla.org/firefox/tracking-protection-always-on/' %} Firefox doesn’t include ad blocking out of the box, but it does include Tracking Protection, which stops sites from installing those tracking cookies we talked about. {% endtrans %}

{% trans url='https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/tracking-protection' %} It’s easy to turn on, and has the added benefit of making Firefox significantly faster. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Why use ad blockers?') }}

{{ _('Ad blockers can also be used to make the web faster.') }}

{{ _('Ads can slow down sites while they load, or get in the way of a streamlined browsing experience.') }}

{{ _('Ads can also be flashy and annoying, distracting you from the stuff you really want to see.') }}

{{ _('“Ads can slow down sites… or get in the way of a streamlined browsing experience.”') }}

{% trans %} Finally, ad companies can track your movements online. It’s called "re-targeting" and it works like this: You visit a site and click on a product. That site puts a small piece of code in your browser called a cookie. When you surf the web, sites will know you clicked on that product and they’ll display ads for it. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Why you might not use an ad blocker') }}

{% trans %} Many media and news organizations make most of their money from advertising. If you block ads on their sites, they won’t get paid. If enough people block ads, business could suffer, and so will the quality of reporting and writing. Luckily, most ad blockers let you make exceptions for your favorite sites. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('How to set Tracking Protection on') }}

{{ _('With Tracking Protection and an ad blocker installed, you’re getting a safe and speedy web experience. Here’s how to get started:') }}

  1. {{ _('Click the menu button and choose Options (or Preferences on a Mac.)') }}
  2. {{ _('Click Privacy & Security on the left and go to the Tracking Protection section.') }}
  3. {{_('Choose between the following options:') }}
    • {{_('Always:') }} {{_('Keep Tracking Protection on as you browse.') }}
    • {{_('Only in private windows:') }} {{_('Turns Tracking Protection on only if you’re using Private Browsing.') }}
    • {{_('Never:') }} {{_('Turns off Tracking Protection for all sites.') }}
