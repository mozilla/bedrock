{{_('Overview')}}

{{_('Mozilla Foundation (“Mozilla” or “Sponsor”) is a global non-profit organization with a mission to promote openness, innovation and opportunity on the Web. In cooperation with the United States National Science Foundation (“NSF”), Mozilla presents this Gigabit Community Fund (the “Fund”) to help foster and support the development of applications running on next-generation networks that could improve people’s everyday lives through creating new education and workforce development opportunities. {{_('Fund Judging')}}

{{_('Evaluation of Submissions will occur during the Proposal Review Period as described below. The decisions of the judges will be final in all instances.')}}

{{_('Fund Proposals')}}

{{_('Each Fund Proposal will be screened initially for three mandatory criteria: (a) Does it focus on education or workforce development; (b) Does it leverage next generation networking technologies like high speed and software defined networking; and (c) Feasibility -- i.e., considering the idea only (not the Participant or Team), could the idea be delivered in the time available in the pilot period. Those Fund Proposals meeting these criteria will be eligible for further review by judges from Mozilla, GENI, NSF, local partners and category-expert reviewers based on the following criteria:')}}

  • {{_('Impact: To what degree could this idea provide significant public benefit, by improving people’s lives? To what extent does the project plan to deliver and evaluate a new learning experience in your community? [35%]')}}
  • {{_('Story: Is the potential public benefit articulated in a clear, compelling way that can excite the general public? [25%]')}}
  • {{_('Innovation: To what degree is this project innovative & likely to be implemented by this team? [25%] ')}}
  • {{_('Advanced networks: To what degree does this project leverage advanced communication & networking technologies, like ultra high speed and Software-Defined Networking? To what degree might this app benefit the community of practice around gigabit apps, through open source and standards? [15%] ')}}

{{_('Participants with Fund Proposals selected to advance to the Pilot Phase will be notified via email at the Captain/Participant address submitted with the initial Submission. If your Fund Proposal is approved, you will be able to log onto the Fund Website to see any comments made by the judges about your Fund Proposal, and to continue your participation in the Pilot Phase of the Challenge.')}}

{{_('For any Fund Proposal accepted for continuation into the Pilot Phase, the Participant will be expected to report on progress via the Fund Website per the methods set forth below, and the judging in the Development Phase will be based exclusively on these progress reports: ')}}

  • {{_('Keep a project blog, occasionally reporting on progress, challenges, and milestones. Participant will be asked to explore empirical impact metrics on the blog and think about how the Participant knows the app is providing public benefit. Incorporating user experience feedback will required.')}}
  • {{_('Develop, log, and share with the Mozilla team an individualized impact/self assessment plan for each participant in the pilot to measure the learning benefit and positive experience for the end user.')}}
  • {{_('Develop in the open to the extent you are willing. We recommend keeping: (i) a roadmap or issue tracker (a wiki, Pivotal tracker, etc.); and (ii) a code repository (i.e. GitHub, Sourceforge, Google Code).')}}
  • {{_('Once per pilot period, each Participant will have the opportunity to make a 10-15 minute presentation and address questions from the audience during a webinar that Sponsor will facilitate.')}}

{{_('Pilot Phase')}}

{{_('The Pilot Phase of the Challenge is scheduled to begin with the Spring Pilot period on or about Tuesday, March 18, 2014, will last a total of 12 weeks ending on June 10, 2014 and will require all documentation & reporting to be finished by the end of the Spring Pilot period. Progress at each Pilot period will be judged based on whether the Participant, in pursuing its approved Fund Proposal, is working effectively to build something compelling and exciting that clearly exemplifies the opportunities a smart 1 Gbps network presents and provides broad public benefit: The two Pilot periods are:')}}

  • {{_('Sprint Pilot period (12 weeks), scheduled to begin on or about Tuesday, March 18, 2014, ending Tuesday, June 10, 2014')}}
  • {{_('Summer Pilot period (12 weeks), scheduled to begin on or about Tuesday, June 25, 2014 ending Tuesday, September 16, 2014')}}

{{_('At the end of each pilot period, each Participant/Team will have the opportunity to make a 10-minute pitch to share: (i) their progress; (ii) where they are going; and (iii) what they think they need to get to the next step. This will take place in an open webinar format where judges and the general public can make comments and ask questions. These webinars will be archived and available for viewing on the Fund Website.')}}

{{_('Fund grant awards will be made available to projects that are deemed worthy and that are making appropriate progress, all as determined by the judges in their absolute discretion. Failure to receive a grant award after any given pilot period does not preclude eligibility from receiving a grant for a future pilot period. However, to continue to be considered for grant support in any later pilot period, Participant must continue to post progress reports to show demonstrable progress toward project goals, and the judges reserve the absolute right, in their discretion, to determine which projects, if any, will be considered for funding in any pilot period.')}}
