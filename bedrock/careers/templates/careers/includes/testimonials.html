It’s hard to come out. First you have to figure out your own stuff. And at some point along the way you have to consider how revealing your authentic self might affect your experience at work or your career trajectory.

My journey to understanding my deeply felt gender identity took many decades. By the time I decided to transition my gender presentation to female, I had already worked at Mozilla for a few years. In that time, I saw a workplace culture where people respected differences and appreciated that diverse ideas from a diverse team leads to better work.

My transition at work was remarkable for how unremarkable it was. I received congratulations, high-fives and hugs from colleagues. Mozilla’s leadership provided invaluable assistance in this process and our health benefits continue to support gender affirming care. Now, five years after I came out at work, I’m thriving at Mozilla. I feel respected and appreciated for what I have to offer as my true self.
