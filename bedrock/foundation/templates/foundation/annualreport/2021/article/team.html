Helen Turvey (King), Mozilla Foundation Board & Chair of Board Compensation Committee
Karim R. Lakhani, Mozilla Corporation Board & Chair of Board Compensation Committee

Much of the content of this report is focused on a singular idea – renewing our investment in Mozilla’s innovation engine. We need to do this because the single most effective way to shape a better internet is by leading through the example of our products and using that to accelerate our policy and advocacy efforts. We are uniquely equipped to meet this moment because of how we are structured — we are at once a global community and two organizations — a non-profit working to ensure the internet remains a public resource that is open and accessible to all and a mission-driven business that creates and supports consumer products in line with our mission.

We can best push against the idea that a better way is “impossible” by building things that prove it is absolutely possible, but only if you put the users of the internet first. To build a team with the skills, experience, vision, and grit to do the “impossible” while going toe-to-toe with the biggest, most well-capitalized companies in the world requires that we attract and retain the best minds from many backgrounds: leading tech companies, activist organizations, and government. We need a global team made of engineers, policy experts, activists, data scientists, marketers, artists, and startup founders.

Our mission-driven focus provides us a critical advantage in that competition, as we offer a natural haven for talent hungry for the opportunity to do their best work on behalf of the users of the internet, rather than for shareholders or other special interests. But that emotional satisfaction is only one half of the compensation equation, we often have to compete with the most well-capitalized companies in the world, who offer compensation both through high salaries and stock.

In order to compete for the talent necessary for us to achieve these ambitious goals, we have developed a compensation strategy that enables us to attract and retain passionate and driven individuals who can bring this vision to life. Both the non-profit Foundation and the for-profit Corporation use a blended data set of compensation from both types of companies in determining the benchmarks for executive compensation, and both organizations use third-party compensation survey data to inform these benchmarks.

At the Foundation, for both executive and non-executive compensation, we benchmark primarily against the non-profit and philanthropic sector (70% weight) blended with data from similarly sized technology companies (30% weight). In Executive compensation at the Corporation we benchmark primarily against mid-sized, for-profit technology companies (70% weight) blended with data from the non-profit and philanthropic sector (30% weight). For non-executive compensation at the Corporation, we benchmark only against the mid-sized, for-profit technology companies, with no blending of non-profit compensation data. We target compensation at the 75th percentile of the blended data for MoFo executives, and the 75th percentile of the blended data for MoCo executives. As a non-profit-owned company, Mozilla Corporation is limited to using cash for performance-based pay.

We apply this same philosophy and benchmarking against the market when it comes to compensating the board members of each entity. Foundation Board members are paid $25k per year for their time, and a small additional amount if they serve on committees. While Corporation Board members are paid $200k per year.

We are always assessing our compensation philosophy to ensure it aligns with our mission and the markets in which we operate – today we have talented folks advancing our mission across the Foundation and Corporation in 31 countries. We expect we will further refine our efforts as we look to expand our ability to better capture opportunities around the world, particularly in Europe and Africa in the coming years.

Mozilla has accomplished a lot of amazing things in the last 25 years, and during our time on the board, we have been able to witness many advancements working to make the internet a healthier and better place. But, we know there is a lot more work to do, and we feel confident this is the team ready to take on the next chapter — and the ones after that.

We aren’t investing in the next quarter, we are investing in the next quarter century — and the next 25 years promise to continue to bring a lot to celebrate online, often thanks to Mozilla.
