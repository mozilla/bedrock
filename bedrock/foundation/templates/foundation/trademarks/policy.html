{{ _('When do I need specific permission to use a Mozilla trademark?') }}

{% trans %}You may do the following without receiving specific permission from Mozilla (or its affiliates):{% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}Use Mozilla wordmarks in text to refer to and/or link to Mozilla and/or applicable Mozilla programs, products, services and technologies.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Use Mozilla logos to link to the applicable programs, products, services and technologies.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Describe a Firefox Add-on in accordance with the Firefox Add-on Guidelines.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Describe a social media account, page, or community in accordance with the Social Media Guidelines.{% endtrans %}

{% trans trademarks="mailto:trademark-permissions@mozilla.com" %}All other uses of a Mozilla trademark require our prior written permission. This includes, without limiting the generality of the above, any use of a Mozilla trademark in a domain name. Contact us at trademark-permissions@mozilla.com. for more information.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('When allowed, how should I use a Mozilla trademark? The General Guidelines') }}

{{ _('Do:') }}

  • {% trans %}Give a Mozilla logo adequate spacing from the other elements on the web page or document (see the Mozilla Logo and Firefox Logo Guidelines below).{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans list=url('foundation.trademarks.list') %}Use the Mozilla trademark exactly as shown in the Trademarks List.{% endtrans %}
    • {% trans %}Don’t abbreviate a Mozilla wordmark or combine it with other words.{% endtrans %}
    • {% trans %}Don’t alter a Mozilla logos design or combine it with other images.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Use Mozilla wordmarks only as an adjective, never as a noun or verb. Do not use them in plural or possessive forms.{% endtrans %}
    • {% trans %}Instead, use the generic term for our product or service following the trademark.{% endtrans %}
    • {% trans %}For example: Firefox web browser, Pocket app.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Include a trademark attribution notice.{% endtrans %}
    • {% trans list=url('foundation.trademarks.list') %}Use a clearly visible notice to provide attribution to Mozilla, such as: “[Mozilla trademark] is a trademark of the Mozilla Foundation in the US and other countries.” Please see the Trademarks List for examples of attribution notices.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('Don’t:') }}

  • {% trans %}Don’t use Mozilla trademarks unless specifically permitted under these General Guidelines or the Additional Guidelines below.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t use Mozilla trademarks in the name of your business, product, service, app, domain name, publication, or other offering.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t use marks, logos, slogans, domain names, or designs that are confusingly similar to Mozilla trademarks.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t use Mozilla trademarks in a way that implies affiliation with, or sponsorship, endorsement, or approval by Mozilla of your products or services.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t display Mozilla trademarks more prominently than your product or service name.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t use Mozilla trademarks on merchandise for sale (e.g., selling t-shirts, mugs, etc.) or other commercial use.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t modify Mozilla’s trademarks, combine them with any other symbols, words, or images, or incorporate them into a tagline or slogan.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t use Mozilla trademarks in a way that is misleading, unfair, defamatory, libelous, disparaging, obscene, or sullies Mozilla’s reputation.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Don’t use Mozilla trademarks in association with any illegal activities or materials.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('Firefox Add-on Guidelines') }}

{% trans %}In addition to the General Guidelines above, your add-on developer name and everything about your add-on (including the add-on name, icon, description, graphics and screenshots) must be unique and free of Mozilla trademarks unless (1) you’ve received prior written permission from Mozilla or (2) you’re only using a Mozilla wordmark to describe that your add-on works with Firefox and as long as your add-on icon and/or name:{% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}Does not suggest or give the impression your add-on is actually published by, affiliated with or endorsed by Mozilla; and{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %} Denotes your own unique identity so as to clearly signal to users that there is no affiliation with or endorsement by Mozilla.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('Social Media Guidelines') }}

{% trans %}In addition to the General Guidelines above, the name and handle of your social media account and any and all pages or communities cannot begin with a Mozilla trademark. In addition, Mozilla logos cannot be used in a way that might suggest affiliation with Mozilla, including, but not limited to, the account, profile, or header images. The only exception to these requirements is if you’ve received prior written permission from Mozilla.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}For example, you cannot name your account, page, or community “Firefox Community” or “Mozilla Software.” However, it would be acceptable to name your account, page, or community “Fans of Firefox” or “Information about Mozilla Software” as long as you do not use the Firefox or Mozilla logos or otherwise suggest any affiliation with Mozilla.{% endtrans %}
