{%- trans link=url('mozorg.about.manifesto') -%} The following five principles stem from the Mozilla Manifesto and inform how we: {%- endtrans -%}

  • {{ _('develop our products and services') }}
  • {{ _('manage user data we collect') }}
  • {{ _('select and interact with partners') }}
  • {{ _('shape our public policy and advocacy work') }}

  1. {{ _('No surprises') }}

    {%- trans -%} Use and share information in a way that is transparent and benefits the user. {%- endtrans -%}

  2. {{ _('User control') }}

    {%- trans -%} Develop products and advocate for best practices that put users in control of their data and online experiences. {%- endtrans -%}

  3. {{ _('Limited data') }}

    {%- trans -%} Collect what we need, de-identify where we can and delete when no longer necessary. {%- endtrans -%}

  4. {{ _('Sensible settings') }}

    {%- trans -%} Design for a thoughtful balance of safety and user experience. {%- endtrans -%}

  5. {{ _('Defense in depth') }}

    {%- trans -%} Maintain multi-layered security controls and practices, many of which are publicly verifiable. {%- endtrans -%}
